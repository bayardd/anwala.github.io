Trump to sign border security bill, declare national emergency - McConnell
1 Min Read
Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell R-KY, speaks to the media at the U.S. Capitol after a tentative deal is set to avert a second partial government shutdown in Washington, U.S., February 12, 2019.      REUTERS/Mary F. Calvert/File Photo
WASHINGTON (Reuters) - U.S. Senate Republican leader Mitch McConnell said on Thursday that President Donald Trump told him he would sign a bipartisan border security bill and at the same time declare a national emergency to make available additional funds for a wall along the southern border.
