New Mexico State closes out Utah Valley for 10th straight win

Jason Groves, Las Cruces Sun-News Published 10:18 p.m. MT Feb. 14, 2019
CONNECT11TWEET11LinkedIn11COMMENT11EMAIL11MORE11

New Mexico State's Terrell Brown splits two Utah Valley defenders as he goes up for a layup during Thursday night's game at the Pan American Center. (Photo11: Jaime Guzman for the Sun-News)

LAS CRUCES - JoJo Zamora took care of the first half and Eli Chuha closed it out to give New Mexico State a 84-77 Western Athletic Conference victory over Utah Valley on Thursday at the Pan American Center.

Zamora was on fire in the first half, scoring all 17 of his points to give the Aggies a two-point halftime lead. Chuha took it from there, scoring 19 of his 21 points in the second half to lead the Aggies to their 10th straight win.

 “Whoever is hot on this team, we are very good at sharing the ball and finding the person with the hot hand," said Chuha, who posted his sixth double-double (fourth in WAC play) with 21 points and 10 rebounds. 

The Aggies improved to 21-4 and 10-1 in WAC play. The Aggies have won at least 10 consecutive games in three straight seasons, four of the last five seasons and five of the past seven seasons. 

"If you are doing this long enough, you want to be the team that everyone is climbing for you," Aggies coach Chris Jans said. "You want to take everyone's best shot because it's going to continue to make you better and more seasoned when you get into March."

After Utah Valley shot 44 percent from the field and 47 percent from 3-point range in the first half, the Aggies clamped down defensively to open the second half.

NM State opened the second half by out scoring Utah Valley 12-3 to open a 50-40 lead.

“The first half, they got every 50-50 ball," Chuha said. "They were playing harder than us in the first half. They are a good team and they fight for everything."

NM State great Jimmy Collins was recognized at halftime on Thursday. (Photo11: Jaime Guzman for the Sun-News)

 

The Aggies were focused defensively on Utah Valley guard Jake Toolson, who scored 26 points against NM State in the first meeting. Toolson was 0 for 4 from the field in the first half and he didn’t score until hitting a 3 to cut the Aggies lead to 56-52. Toolson finished with six points on 2 of 9 shooting.

“It's pick your poison with them," Jans said. "The game plan was to figure out a way to slow (Toolson) down and live with some 3s from other guys. He is playing at a Player of the Year type numbers, but we stayed with it."

After shooting 7 of 15 from 3-point range in the first half, Utah Valley was 4 of 14 (28.6 percent) from long range in the second half.

Zamora made his first four field goal attempts and was 5 for 5 from 3-point range in the first half for 17 points to lead the Aggies to a 39-37 halftime lead.

"I always told him to keep his spirits up and don't let his confidence go down and tonight was one of his nights," Chuha said. 

Zamora, who had scored eight total points in the Aggies previous three games, was 0 for 2 in seven minutes in the second half, but Trevelin Queen and Terrell Brown each scored 11 points. 

“We needed (Zamora) to break out," Jans said. "Obviously we want him to be more consistent. He's such a talented scorer, and for whatever reason, he has had a hard time triggering it. 

"In the second half, he had a couple shots early and they didn't go in and couldn't get it going."

The Aggies made eight 3s in the first half, but Utah Valley was just as deadly from long range, hitting 7 of 15 from 3-point range.

NM State finished the game 47 percent from the field and 11 of 28 (39.3 percent) from 3-point range.

The Aggies host 1-8 Seattle on Saturday, but as Missouri-Kansas City’s victory over Grand Canyon on Thursday proved, anything can happen in WAC play.

“Anybody is capable of beating anybody so you can't ever relax," Chuha said.
