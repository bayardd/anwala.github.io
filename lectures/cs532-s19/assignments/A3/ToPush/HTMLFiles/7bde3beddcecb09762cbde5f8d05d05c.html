Greedy Williams provides smiles on Valentine's Day at Shriners Hospital

  • Daniel Brown

    Daniel Brown

    Sports MMJ

  • Feb 14, 2019
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Print
  • Save

SHREVEPORT, La. -- Valentine's Day conjures up thoughts of cupid and candy, but for former LSU cornerback and future NFL first-round draft pick Greedy Williams, this thought was on community. That's why he decided to return to his hometown and give some love at the Shriners Hospitals for Children.

"You know, just wanting to do something, wanting to do something in the city. So we came up with let's go to a hospital and during Valentine's Day because that's a day of love and so we had that idea and came up with teddy bears, then next came the candy, then next let's dress them up. So Build-A-Bear, shoutout to Build-A-Bear," Williams explained.

He added, "You know it just means the world. Coming out of training, coming back to do something like this, that was big. Just watching the kids smile, my job is to come back and make kids smile and just to give them hope and that's something I think I accomplished today."

Williams is a busy man from here on out as he returns to training for the NFL combine where he hopes to eventually be selected within the top five picks of the upcoming NFL draft.
