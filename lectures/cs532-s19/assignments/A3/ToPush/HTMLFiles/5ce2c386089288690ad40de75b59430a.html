Gregory-Portland baseball team ready for chance at playoffs in new district

Len Hayward, Corpus Christi Caller Times Published 2:23 p.m. CT Feb. 14, 2019 | Updated 2:24 p.m. CT Feb. 14, 2019
CONNECT11TWEET11LinkedIn11COMMENT11EMAIL11MORE11

PORTLAND — The Gregory-Portland baseball team looked relaxed on Tuesday as the sun was setting.

Buy Photo

Gregory-Portland's Isaac Ponce pitches the ball during their game against Calallen on Tuesday, April 17, 2018 at Gregory-Portland. (Photo11: Casey Jackson/Caller-Times)

The Wildcats were joking with each other during a hitting drill, and third-year coach Lance Standley was offering some advice as the hitters took cuts.

The team was still working on situations and hitting fundamentals. It was evident that this group has confidence in each other and their coach, and in their ability to break through to the playoffs for the first time since 2015.

“Everyone is building each other, and all have confidence in each other,” said senior infielder Ryan Starks. “We believe we are going to come through this year.”

Standley said in his third year in the program, players are more relaxed, knowing his expectations and coaching style.

“You stay playing a little more relaxed,” Standley said. “It’s like I’ve got more coaches on the field. When you start feeling better and understanding things, you are not afraid to make mistakes and start playing better.”

The Wildcats qualified for the 30-5A zone playoffs in 2018 but lost to eventual state tournament qualifier Veterans Memorial. The experienced gained from that series has gone a long way in helping build confidence.

Plus the team is in a new district after the split up of the old 30-5A, and joins the smaller 29-5A, which has seven teams instead of 11.

“We fell in a great district right now, but I do feel 11 teams is a lot harder than seven,” Standley said with a chuckle.

Stark said the change in district takes some pressure off and the loss last year to the Eagles has helped in early season workouts in 2019.

“We know where we needed to get better, and how we needed to get better,” Stark said. “Don’t get us wrong, in baseball anything can happen but a lot of the pressure is kind of relief that we don’t have that big, huge district.”

INFOBOX

GREGORY-PORTLAND WILDCATS

2018 record: 18-8-3

Top returning players: IF/P Isaac Ponce, sr.; IF Ryan Stark, sr.; OF/P Alex Aguirre, sr.

What to expect in 2019: The loss of All-South Texas and All-District player Austin Ochoa forced third-year coach Lance Standley to do some maneuvering with his infield and lineup heading into this season but the team returns five starters, and could take advantage of the new district alignment. Ponce is one of the area’s best pitchers after compiling a 6-3 record with a 2.56 ERA. Stark, Aguirre and Ponce all bring back solid bats for their lineup.

Follow caller.com and the Caller-Times as we tour area high school baseball practices, and check out “HARDBALL”, our annual high school baseball-softball preview section on Feb. 24.
