Shea Weber reached a round number Thursday at Bridgestone Arena.

And he received a standing ovation from the crowd.

The Canadiens defenseman and former Predators captain, who was traded for P.K. Subban after the 2015-16 season, played in his 900th career regular-season game.

Weber played 763 of those games with the Predators.

    "I'm not really thinking about that tonight," Weber said. "It’s pretty good accomplishment, but the biggest thing for us is making sure were playing a good solid game tonight and getting two points. Last time they played us in our building they suffocated us and played really well."

    The Predators got the last laugh, though, with a 3-1 victory, the Canadiens' second loss in regulation in 12 games and Nashville's first victory in four tries.

    Ryan Hartman scored for the first time since Dec. 13 — after a span of 27 games without a goal — to give the Predators a 1-0 lead with 10 minutes, 11 seconds left in the second. 

    Hartman mimicked tossing a monkey off his back on is way to the bench after scoring.

    Tomas Tatar tied the score at 14:34 of the third on the Canadiens' 33rd shot on goal of the game.

    But Brian Boyle's first goal as a Predator put the home team ahead 2-1 in the third. Viktor Arvidsson quickly followed with his 26th goal to make it 3-1.

    CLOSE

    Predators forward Viktor Arvidsson on victory against the Canadiens Paul Skrbina, The Tennessean

    Pekka Rinne was better than solid in net for the Predators, making 35 saves. 

    Weber had 166 goals and 277 assists in 763 regular-season games with the Predators.

    Weber and Subban faced each other at Bridgestone for the first time since the trade.

    When asked whether it would be weird playing at his former home rink, Weber said, "A bit, but not as bad as it was a couple years ago."

    The Predators took 7-0 shots on goal lead during the first 2:30 of the first period. The Canadiens came up empty despite having the first 10 during the second period.

    Arvidsson nearly made it 2-0 with less than three minutes left in the third, but was stopped by Carey Price on a breakaway.
